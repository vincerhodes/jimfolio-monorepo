Mandarin Tones

Mandarin Chinese has 4 tones (+ a neutral tone). The same syllable spoken in different tones has completely different meanings.

1

First Tone — Flat & High

High level, sustained pitch

妈

mā (mother)

书

shū (book)

飞

fēi (fly)

2

Second Tone — Rising

Rising, like asking a question

麻

má (hemp)

来

lái (come)

人

rén (person)

3

Third Tone — Dipping

Falls then rises (V-shape)

马

mǎ (horse)

你

nǐ (you)

好

hǎo (good)

4

Fourth Tone — Falling

Sharp downward, like a command

骂

mà (scold)

大

dà (big)

是

shì (is)

0

Neutral Tone — Light & Short

Brief, unstressed syllable. No tone mark written.

吗

ma (question)

的

de (particle)

呢

ne (and you?)

Numbers & Counting

Chinese numbers follow a logical pattern. Master 1–10 and you can count to 99!

Tens & Hundreds

How to Form Numbers

11 → 十一 (shí yī)

ten + one

35 → 三十五 (sān shí wǔ)

three + ten + five

100 → 一百 (yī bǎi)

one + hundred

2025 → 二零二五 (èr líng èr wǔ)

Years are read digit by digit
